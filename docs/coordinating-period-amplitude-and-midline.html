    Definition 2.5.1.

    For a function \(f\text{,}\) the period is the smallest positive value \(t\) such that for all \(x\) in the domain, \(f(x+t)=f(x)\text{.}\)
    ðŸ”—
    Informally, the period of a trigonometric function is the distance it takes to complete one cycle, before it begins to repeat.
